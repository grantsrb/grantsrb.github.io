About Me and the Blog

I'm currently a 3rd year PhD student in Jay McClelland's lab. I study numeric cognition and spatial reasoning using multimodal and embodied neural network models. My interests lie at the intersection of Psychology, Neuroscience, and Artificial Intelligence, generally in the broad, nebulously defined pursuit of understanding intelligence.

This blog is mainly to showcase my projects that were promising or interesting but not worth publishing. I named this blog, "Project Purgatory" because these projects usually haven't made it to publishable heaven, but they weren't bad enough to be scrapped in project hell. Some of the projects find themselves listed here because I'm currently developing them further, or I couldn't find the time to pursue them further, or someone else beat me to the punch. For each project, I'll link to the repo/writeup and describe what I'm doing to make it better, or why I won't be pursuing it further.
